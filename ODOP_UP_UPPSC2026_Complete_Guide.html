🎯 Core Objectives
  • Preserve and promote local crafts, skills, and traditional art forms district-wise
  • Generate employment for 25 lakh youth across UP within 5 years
  • Improve artisan income and reduce rural-to-urban migration
  • Adopt modern technology for quality improvement and competitiveness
  • Connect UP's traditional products with national and international markets
  • Help revive industries on verge of extinction via GI tagging and branding
  • Contribute 2% increase to state GDP through MSME growth
⚙️ Four Components of ODOP Scheme
1. Margin Money Scheme
25% subsidy on project cost (max ₹25 lakh) for artisans/MSMEs. Apply at diupmsme.upsdc.gov.in. Covers machinery, working capital.
2. Training & Toolkit Scheme
10-day free training + ₹200/day stipend + free advanced toolkit for unskilled artisans. ~20,000 artisans trained per year statewide.
3. Common Facility Centres (CFCs)
200+ CFCs sanctioned with modern machinery for shared use. Reduces individual capital burden. Improves quality standards for exports.
4. Marketing Assistance
ODOP Bazaar, GeM portal, Amazon/Flipkart/eBay MoUs. National & international trade fair participation. ODOP Walls in Singapore, Milan, New York.

⚖️ GI Tag Legal Framework
  • Act: Geographical Indications of Goods (Registration & Protection) Act, 1999
  • Registry: GI Registry, Chennai (under DPIIT, Ministry of Commerce & Industry)
  • WTO Agreement: TRIPS (Trade-Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights)
  • Purpose: Certifies product originates from specific region & has qualities attributable to it
  • ODOP scheme and GI tagging work together to protect artisan identity and prevent imitation
  • Moradabad = "Peetal Nagri" (Brass City), Kannauj = "Perfume Capital of India"

    ⚠️ Key Challenges
    Quality Standardisation
    Inconsistent product quality across artisans; lack of uniform quality standards for international buyers.
    Technology Adoption
    Many artisans resist modern tools; digital literacy gaps limit e-commerce access in rural areas.
    Credit Access Gaps
    Small artisans lack collateral for bank loans; margin money scheme has implementation delays in some districts.
    Uneven Geographic Spread
    Benefits concentrated in famous clusters (Varanasi, Agra, Lucknow, Moradabad); remote districts underserved.
    Imitation Products
    Machine-made replicas undercut genuine GI-tagged products. Chikankari, Banarasi silk most affected.
    Packaging & Branding
    Artisans lack skills in modern packaging and brand storytelling needed for premium/international markets.
    ✅ Success Stories
    Kalanamak Rice — Siddharthnagar
    Cultivation: 2,000 ha → 20,000 ha. GI-tagged 2013. Gifted at G20 2023. Bronze at ODOP Awards 2024. Farmer income doubled.
    Chikankari — Lucknow
    Cottage craft → ₹10,000+ crore industry. 2.5 lakh artisans (mostly women). GI-tagged. Exported globally.
    Terracotta — Gorakhpur
    Near-extinct craft revived under ODOP. National awards. Now exported to Europe and the USA.
    Sports Goods — Meerut
    ~70% of India's cricket bats made here. Major export hub. CFCs with quality-testing labs established.
    Pilibhit Flute
    90% of India's bamboo flutes from Pilibhit. GI-tagged. Unique identity created via ODOP branding.
    Banarasi Silk — Varanasi
    500+ year tradition. ~30,000 weavers. GI-tagged. Gifted at G7 and G20 summits by PM Modi.
    🔗 Similar/Related Schemes
    PM FME (Pradhan Mantri Formalisation of Micro Food Processing Enterprises) — Central scheme, 35% subsidy on machinery for food processing MSMEs. Complements ODOP food product districts.
    Districts as Export Hubs (DEH) — DPIIT initiative. ODOP products integrated for export promotion. GI-tagged products prioritised for DEH.
    Cluster Development Programme (CDP) — Ministry of MSME. Funds soft & hard infrastructure in ODOP clusters. Shared CFCs funded under CDP.
    Vocal for Local / Aatmanirbhar Bharat — ODOP aligns with PM Modi's vision. ODOP products promoted as symbols of local self-reliance nationally and internationally.
    Similar State Schemes — Bihar, Madhya Pradesh (Silver 2024), Maharashtra, Punjab, Rajasthan all have ODOP-type initiatives. UP remains the pioneer and model state.
    Japan OVOP Influence — Japan's "One Village One Product" (Dr. Morihiko Hiramatsu, Oita Prefecture, 1979) is the direct inspiration for India's ODOP.
