📚 Constitutional Provisions — Article 168–212
  • UP is one of 6 states with Vidhan Parishad (AN Pradesh, Bihar, Karnataka, Maharashtra, Telangana, UP)
  • Max members = 1/3rd of Vidhan Sabha (not less than 40) — Art. 171
  • Established by Government of India Act, 1935
  • First session: 29 July 1937
  • First Chairman: Sir Sitaram (1937); First Vice-Chairperson: Begum Aijaz Rasul
  • Parishad cannot be dissolved — permanent body
  • Money Bill: Parishad has no veto — can delay 14 days only
  • Principal Secretary: Dr. Rajesh Singh, IAS

👥 Key Officials & Their Roles
🏅 District Magistrate (DM) / Collector
IAS officer · Head of district civil administration · Law & order + Revenue + Development coordination · Presides over DCC (District Coordination Committee) · Election Returning Officer · Chairman District Disaster Management Authority
🚔 Superintendent of Police (SP)
IPS officer · Head of district police · Law & order, crime prevention · Reports to DIG/IG of Range · In major cities: Commissioner of Police system (Lucknow, Kanpur, Agra, Varanasi, Prayagraj, Ghaziabad, Noida)
📋 SDM (Sub-Divisional Magistrate)
PCS officer · Executive Magistrate powers · Revenue matters at tehsil level · Land records, mutation, jamabandi · Conducts local body elections at tehsil level · Direct career entry point for UPPSC candidates!
🏛️ Commissioner System in UP — Divisional Commissioner
  • IAS officer of Joint Secretary rank or above
  • Heads the Mandal (Division) — supervisory authority over DMs
  • Hears revision/appeals against DM orders (Revenue cases)
  • Appellate authority for land acquisition, land records disputes
  • Coordinates law & order at divisional level with DIG Police
  • Commissioner = Mandlayukt in Hindi
  • Has additional charge of various development boards in division
  • Real power center: DM has more day-to-day authority
  • Commissioner of Police (Urban): Separate system in 8 major cities
  • UP uses Commissioner + DM parallel system (not Commissioner-only like Delhi)

📜 73rd Amendment & UP Context
  • 73rd CAA 1992 → Art. 243A–243O: Constitutional status to PRIs
  • UP amended UP Panchayat Raj Act accordingly in 1994
  • UP Panchayati Raj Act, 1947: Principal legislation
  • UP Kshettra Panchayats & Zila Panchayats Adhiniyam, 1961: Governs 2nd & 3rd tiers
  • Election conducted by: UP State Election Commission
  • Reservations: 1/3rd for women (UP has 33% minimum; many areas 50%)
  • OBC reservation: Rotating basis, max 27%
  • SC/ST: Proportionate to population

Urban Local Bodies — नगर निकाय

74th Amendment · UP Nagar Palika Adhiniyam 1916 (amended 1994)
🏙️
NAGAR NIGAM
Municipal Corporation
  • Population: >3 Lakh
  • Head: Mayor (directly elected)
  • CEO: Nagar Ayukt (IAS)
  • Body: General Board
  • UP has 17 Nagar Nigams
  • Largest: Kanpur Nagar Nigam
  • Powers: All municipal functions incl. town planning
🏘️
NAGAR PALIKA PARISHAD
Municipal Council
  • Population: 30,000–3 Lakh
  • Head: Adhyaksh (directly elected)
  • CEO: Executive Officer (EO)
  • UP has 200 NPPs approx.
  • Medium-sized towns
  • Limited town planning powers
🏡
NAGAR PANCHAYAT
Town Area Committee
  • Population: 11,000–30,000
  • Head: Adhyaksh
  • CEO: Executive Officer
  • UP has ~438 Nagar Panchayats
  • Transitional areas (rural-to-urban)
  • Basic civic services only
📚 Important ULB Facts for UPPSC
  • 74th Amendment 1992: Art. 243P–243ZG — Constitutional status to ULBs
  • 12th Schedule: 18 functions for ULBs
  • Election: UP State Election Commission
  • UP's 17 Nagar Nigams include: Lucknow, Kanpur, Agra, Varanasi, Prayagraj, Meerut, Bareilly, Aligarh, Moradabad, Ghaziabad, Gorakhpur, Firozabad, Saharanpur, Mathura-Vrindavan, Jhansi, Ayodhya, Noida
  • Cantonment Boards: Under Union Government (not state)
  • Development Authorities: LDA (Lucknow), ADA (Agra), VDA (Varanasi) — Town planning bodies
  • UIDA: UP Industrial Development Authority
  • Special provisions: Smart City Mission covers Lucknow, Varanasi, Kanpur, Agra, Bareilly, Aligarh, Saharanpur, Moradabad

⚖️ Key Facts
  • Official Name: High Court of Judicature at Allahabad
  • Principal Seat: Prayagraj (Allahabad) — covers ~60 districts
  • Permanent Bench: Lucknow — covers ~15 districts (Lucknow, Hardoi, Kheri, Rae Bareli, Sitapur, Unnao, Faizabad/Ayodhya, Ambedkar Nagar, Bahraich, Shravasti, Barabanki, Gonda, Balrampur, Pratapgarh, Sultanpur)
  • Jurisdiction: Entire UP (75 districts) + Lucknow bench specific districts
  • Largest HC in India by sanctioned judge strength: 160 judges
  • Mandatory retirement age: 62 years
  • Established originally in Agra, moved to Allahabad in 1869
👤 Current Chief Justice
⚖️
Justice Arun Bhansali
Chief Justice, Allahabad High Court
Since 5 Feb 2024
📚 Lucknow Bench Origin
Lucknow Bench established by Oudh Civil Courts Act, 1925. The Chief Court of Oudh was merged with Allahabad HC on 25 February 1948. Lucknow bench is a permanent circuit bench, not a separate High Court.
📜 Constitutional Provisions (Art. 214–231)
  • Art. 214: Every state shall have an HC
  • Art. 215: HC is Court of Record
  • Art. 217: Appointment of HC judges — President consults CJI + Governor + HC Chief Justice
  • Art. 226: Writ jurisdiction (broader than SC's Art. 32)
  • Art. 227: Superintendence over all courts in state
  • Uttarakhand was under Allahabad HC before 2000; Uttarakhand HC established 9 Nov 2000
  • Allahabad HC has jurisdiction over all civil, criminal, revenue courts in UP
  • Important judgments on UP: Babri Masjid (2010), IAS transfers, Panchayati Raj elections, UPPSC exam matters
  • UP State Law Commission advises on legal reforms

🔵 Commissionerate System (Urban)
  • 8 cities have Commissioner of Police system: Lucknow, Kanpur, Agra, Varanasi, Prayagraj, Ghaziabad, Gautam Buddha Nagar (Noida), Gorakhpur

  • PAC (Provincial Armed Constabulary) — UP's armed police reserve
  • STF (Special Task Force) — Organized crime, NDPS
  • ATS (Anti-Terrorism Squad) — Counter-terrorism

  • CCTNS (Crime & Criminal Tracking Network) implementation
  • DIAL 112 — Emergency Response Support System
  • UP Police Bhavan — new HQ, Lucknow
  • UPCOP App for FIR filing & citizen services

UP Vidhan Sabha में कुल कितनी सीटें हैं, तथा SC के लिए कितनी सीटें आरक्षित हैं?
(A) 400 सीटें, 80 SC आरक्षित
(B) 403 सीटें, 84 SC आरक्षित
(C) 403 सीटें, 90 SC आरक्षित

(A) 17 Mandals, 75 Districts, 800 Blocks

(C) 18 Mandals, 75 Districts, 821 Blocks
(D) 18 Mandals, 75 Districts, 850 Blocks
