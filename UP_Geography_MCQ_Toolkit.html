💧 Rivers — Tributaries & Confluences
1
Medium UPPSC 2023 Type
Q1. Ram Ganga river joins the Ganga at which place?
✅ Correct Answer: C — Kannauj

Ram Ganga originates from Namik Glacier in Uttarakhand, flows through Corbett NP, enters UP, and meets the Ganga near Kannauj (Kannauj district, Kannauj division). Note: Betwa joins Yamuna near Hamirpur, NOT Prayagraj — a common MCQ trap.

🗺️ Map Tip RAM-GANGA → KANnauj: "RAMs always KAAN (ears) bade hote hain" — Kannauj se milti hai.
2
Hard
Q2. River Betwa joins the Yamuna near which place in Uttar Pradesh?
✅ Correct Answer: B — Hamirpur

Betwa (Vetravati) originates from Vindhya hills in MP, enters UP, and joins the Yamuna near Hamirpur (Hamirpur district, Chitrakoot division). This is a frequently asked fact — UPPSC 2023 Paper I had a statement about Betwa joining at Prayagraj, which was FALSE.

🗺️ Map Tip Betwa = Bundelkhand ki naadi. B for Betwa, B for Bundelkhand, B for Hamirpur (think "Betwa-Bundelkhand-HAMmer" — Hamirpur).
3
Hard
Q3. Which of the following rivers flows through the Terai region of UP and finally joins the Ghaghara?
1. Rapti   2. Sharda   3. Gomti   4. Tons
Select correct answer:
✅ Correct Answer: A — 1 and 2 only (Rapti & Sharda)

Rapti (originates Nepal, flows through Gorakhpur) and Sharda/Saryu (flows from Kumaon Himalayas) both join the Ghaghara. Gomti joins the Ganga at Ghazipur, NOT Ghaghara. Tons is a tributary of Yamuna (joins at Prayagraj area).

🗺️ Map Tip Ghaghara's tributaries: "RAPTI aur SHARDA dono GHAGHARA mein jaati hain" — remember RS → G. Gomti alag — Ganga mein milti hai Ghazipur pe.
4
Medium
Q4. River Son (Sone) originates from which location and joins Ganga at?
✅ Correct Answer: C — Amarkantak → joins at Arrah (Bihar)

Son river originates from Amarkantak plateau (MP) — same source as Narmada & Johila. It flows northeast through MP, enters UP's Sonbhadra district (Vindhya region), then Bihar, finally joining Ganga near Arrah/Dinapur (Bihar). Note: Son forms part of UP's southern boundary.

🗺️ Map Tip "AMAR ke teen bete: SON, NARMADA, JOHILA" — all three from Amarkantak. Son goes NORTH-EAST (Bihar), Narmada goes WEST (Arabian Sea).
🏗️ Dams & Barrages of UP
5
Medium UPPSC 2022 Direct
Q5. Which one of the following dams is NOT located in Uttar Pradesh?
✅ Correct Answer: C — Baghelkhand Dam

"Baghelkhand" is a region name, not a specific dam in UP. Badua Dam (Mirzapur, on Badua river), Adwa Dam (Sonbhadra), and Ahraura Dam (Mirzapur) are all in UP's Vindhya region. This was directly asked in UPPSC 2022!

🗺️ Map Tip UP ke Vindhya dams: "BAD-ADW-AHRA" → Badua, Adwa, Ahraura — all Mirzapur-Sonbhadra belt. Baghelkhand = region (MP-UP border area).
6
Hard
Q6. Rihand Dam (Govind Ballabh Pant Sagar) is constructed on which river and is located in which district of UP?
✅ Correct Answer: B — Rihand river — Sonbhadra

Rihand Dam (officially Govind Ballabh Pant Sagar) is India's largest man-made lake. It's built on the Rihand river (tributary of Son) in Sonbhadra district (Varanasi division). Capacity: ~10.6 billion m³. The Renukoot/Renusagar industrial area (HINDALCO aluminium) uses this power.

🗺️ Map Tip RIHAND = SONBHADRA = India's largest reservoir. "SONO ke SON mein RIHAND" — Sonbhadra district, Son river ka tributary. GBP Sagar = GB Pant = UP ke hi governor ka naam.
7
Medium
Q7. Matatila Dam is built on which river and which state/s share this reservoir?
✅ Correct Answer: B — Betwa river — UP & MP

Matatila Dam is on the Betwa river, located in Lalitpur district (Jhansi division, UP), shared with Madhya Pradesh. It's a key irrigation dam for Bundelkhand. Similarly, Rajghat Dam is also on Betwa (UP-MP border). Ken river has Gangau & Bariyarpur dams.

🗺️ Map Tip Bundelkhand dams order West→East: "MATATILA-RAJGHAT-PARICHHA" all on Betwa. Matatila = Lalitpur (southernmost UP, touches MP).
8
Hard
Q8. Which of the following barrages on Ganga in UP are correctly matched with their districts?
1. Narora Barrage — Bulandshahr   2. Kanpur Barrage — Unnao   3. Farakka Barrage — Varanasi
✅ Correct Answer: A — Only 1

Narora Barrage is in Bulandshahr district (Aligarh division) ✅. Kanpur Barrage is in Kanpur Nagar district itself, not Unnao ❌. Farakka Barrage is in West Bengal (Murshidabad), NOT in UP ❌. Narora is also home to India's first Nuclear Power Station (NAPS).

🗺️ Map Tip Narora = Bulandshahr = NAPS (Nuclear + Barrage). "NARO-NAPS-BULA" — Narora Atomic Power Station, Bulandshahr. Farakka is WB — far from UP!

🗺️ Divisional & District Geography
9
Medium UPPSC 2025 Type
Q9. Which district of Uttar Pradesh shares its boundary with only one other district of UP?
✅ Correct Answer: D — None of the above (trick question from UPPSC 2025)

UPPSC 2025 Paper I asked: which districts share boundary with only ONE other UP district? Answer was none of Lalitpur, Saharanpur, or Sonbhadra — all share with multiple UP districts + other states. Sonbhadra touches MP, Jharkhand, Bihar, Chhattisgarh. Saharanpur touches Uttarakhand, Haryana + multiple UP districts. Lalitpur touches MP + Jhansi, Mahoba, Hamirpur.

🗺️ Map Tip Sonbhadra = "Chaar state ka neighbour" — MP, Jharkhand, Bihar, Chhattisgarh — plus UP districts. Most multi-bordered district of UP.
10
Hard
Q10. How many divisions does Uttar Pradesh currently have, and which is the most recently created division?
✅ Correct Answer: B — 18 divisions, Chitrakoot (2008)

UP has 18 Mandals (divisions) and 75 districts. The 18th and most recently created division is Chitrakoot Mandal (2008), comprising Chitrakoot, Banda, Hamirpur, Mahoba districts. Faizabad was renamed Ayodhya division but was not newly created — it was renamed.

🗺️ Map Tip 18 Mandals mnemonic: "ALMA-VARS-BAGE-KAJI-AZFU-DEVA-CHIT-MIRA" (first letters of all 18: Agra, Lucknow, Mathura-area etc.) — or remember 75 districts, 18 divisions, 1 state capital (Lucknow).
11
Medium
Q11. Prayagraj division consists of how many districts, and which district was added to make it a division?
✅ Correct Answer: B — 4 districts

Prayagraj (Allahabad) Mandal has 4 districts: Prayagraj, Fatehpur, Kaushambi, and Pratapgarh. Prayagraj is also the HQ of Allahabad High Court (largest high court in India). Mirzapur is in Vindhyachal division.

🗺️ Map Tip Prayagraj division: "PFKP" = Prayagraj, Fatehpur, Kaushambi, Pratapgarh. "Prayagraj ke FOUR sangam" — Ganga, Yamuna, Saraswati + 4th district Pratapgarh!
12
Hard
Q12. Which of the following UP districts does NOT share an international boundary (with Nepal)?
✅ Correct Answer: C — Sitapur

UP shares ~579 km border with Nepal. Districts sharing Nepal boundary (west to east): Pilibhit, Lakhimpur Kheri, Bahraich, Shravasti, Balrampur, Siddharthnagar, Maharajganj. That's 7 districts. Sitapur is an interior district (Lucknow division) — does NOT touch Nepal.

🗺️ Map Tip Nepal border UP districts: "PIL-LAKH-BAH-SHRA-BAL-SID-MAH" = Pilibhit, Lakhimpur, Bahraich, Shravasti, Balrampur, Siddharthnagar, Maharajganj. 7 districts — remember "P-L-B-S-B-S-M".
13
Medium
Q13. Vindhyachal division of UP includes which of the following districts?
✅ Correct Answer: C — Mirzapur, Sonbhadra, Chandauli

Vindhyachal Division (HQ: Mirzapur) has 3 districts: Mirzapur, Sonbhadra, Chandauli. This is the mineral-rich division of UP (coal, limestone, bauxite in Sonbhadra; stone quarries in Mirzapur). Banda is in Chitrakoot division.

🗺️ Map Tip "VINDHYA-MSC" = Mirzapur, Sonbhadra, Chandauli. Think "Mountains-Stones-Coal" = UP's southernmost mineral belt.
14
Hard
Q14. Which UP district was created most recently (2011) as a new district carved out of Muzaffarnagar?
✅ Correct Answer: B — Shamli (2011)

Shamli was carved from Muzaffarnagar district in 2011, becoming UP's 72nd district at the time. It is in Saharanpur division. The 4 newest districts (all 2011): Shamli (from Muzaffarnagar), Hapur (from Ghaziabad), Sambhal (from Moradabad), Amethi (from Sultanpur). UP currently has 75 districts.

🗺️ Map Tip 2011 New 4 districts: "SHHA-SA" = SHamli, HApur, SAmbhal, Amethi. Shamli = Muzaffarnagar se katai. Hapur = Ghaziabad se katai.

🐯 Wildlife Sanctuaries & National Parks

5 Questions · 2 Medium3 Hard

🌿 National Parks & Wildlife Sanctuaries of UP
15
Medium UPPSC 2022 Direct
Q15. Dudhwa National Park is situated in which district of Uttar Pradesh?
✅ Correct Answer: B — Lakhimpur Kheri

Dudhwa National Park (declared NP in 1977) is in Lakhimpur Kheri district, Lucknow division. Along with Katerniaghat WLS (Bahraich), they form the Dudhwa Tiger Reserve. Famous for: one-horned rhinos (reintroduced), swamp deer (Barasingha), tigers. Billy Arjan Singh conserved this area.

🗺️ Map Tip "DUDHWA = KHEER" mnemonic — Dudhwa (milk/dudh) → KHERI (Lakhimpur Kheri). Always a trap to put Bahraich or Pilibhit as options!
16
Hard
Q16. Chandraprabha Wildlife Sanctuary is located in which district of UP?
✅ Correct Answer: B — Chandauli

Chandraprabha Wildlife Sanctuary is in Chandauli district (Vindhyachal division). It's named after the Chandra Prabha river (tributary of Ganga). The sanctuary was the site of the first lion reintroduction in India (1957 — lions from Gujarat's Gir). Lions couldn't survive and went extinct there. This was directly asked in UPPSC 2023!

🗺️ Map Tip "CHANDRA = CHANDAULI" — same word root. Chandraprabha river flows through Chandauli = sanctuary there. Sonbhadra trap — close neighbor but wrong.
17
Hard
Q17. Pilibhit Tiger Reserve — which of the following statements are correct?
1. It shares border with Nepal.   2. Sharda river flows through it.   3. It was declared Tiger Reserve in 2014.
✅ Correct Answer: D — All three are correct

Pilibhit Tiger Reserve (2014): ✅ Shares border with Nepal (Pilibhit district is on Nepal border). ✅ Sharda, Khakra rivers flow through it. ✅ Declared India's 47th Tiger Reserve in 2014. Area: ~730 sq km. Part of Terai Arc Landscape. Connected to Dudhwa and Nepal's Shuklaphanta NP.

🗺️ Map Tip Pilibhit = "PIL-BOT" — PIL (border) + BHT (Bharat-Nepal touching). 2014 Tiger Reserve. Terai mein hai, Nepal se lagti hai.
18
Medium
Q18. Hastinapur Wildlife Sanctuary is spread across which districts of UP?
✅ Correct Answer: B — Meerut, Hapur, Amroha, Bijnor, Muzaffarnagar

Hastinapur Wildlife Sanctuary (2085 sq km) is UP's largest wildlife sanctuary, spread across 5 districts: Meerut, Hapur, Amroha (J.P. Nagar), Bijnor, Muzaffarnagar. Located along the Ganga-Ramganga doab. Famous for: Gangetic dolphins, gharials, swamp deer. Part of the floodplain ecosystem.

🗺️ Map Tip Hastinapur = Mahabharata ki nagari = Western UP. 5 districts along Ganga-Ram Ganga doab. "MHAMB" = Meerut, Hapur, Amroha, Muzaffarnagar, Bijnor.
19
Hard
Q19. Which wildlife sanctuary of UP is known for the highest density of gharials and is located in Chambal river valley?
✅ Correct Answer: C — National Chambal Sanctuary

National Chambal Wildlife Sanctuary is a tristate sanctuary (UP-MP-Rajasthan) along Chambal river. UP's part covers Agra, Etawah, Auraiya, Jalaun districts. Famous for: Gharials (critically endangered), Gangetic river dolphins, Red-crowned roof turtles, Skimmers. This is a linear riverine sanctuary ~400 km long.

🗺️ Map Tip Chambal = "Gharial ka ghar" = Agra-Etawah belt. 3 states: UP (Agra/Etawah) + MP (Morena) + Rajasthan (Kota). Okhla = birds only (Gautam Budh Nagar, Delhi border).

🌾 Agro-Climatic Zones, Minerals & Crops

6 Questions · 3 Medium3 Hard

🌡️ Agro-Climatic Zones (9 Zones)
20
Medium
Q20. How many Agro-Climatic Zones does Uttar Pradesh have, and which zone covers the maximum area?
✅ Correct Answer: B — 9 zones, Central Plain zone (Zone VI)

UP has 9 Agro-Climatic Zones: (I) Bhabar & Tarai, (II) Western Plain, (III) Mid-Western Plain, (IV) South-Western Semi-Arid, (V) Central-Western Plain, (VI) Bundelkhand, (VII) North-Eastern Plain, (VIII) Eastern Plain, (IX) Vindhyan. Central Plain zone covers maximum area. Bundelkhand (VI) is most drought-prone.

🗺️ Map Tip 9 zones — remember North→South: Bhabar-Terai (top) → Western Plains → Semi-Arid → Central → Bundelkhand (drought) → Eastern → Vindhyan (bottom). Like layers of a sandwich!
21
Hard
Q21. Which agro-climatic zone of UP is known as the "sugarcane bowl" and corresponds roughly to which region?
✅ Correct Answer: B — Zone II — Western Plain

Western Plain Zone (Meerut, Muzaffarnagar, Saharanpur, Bijnor, Baghpat, Hapur, Shamli) is UP's sugarcane hub — hence "sugarcane bowl." UP produces ~40-45% of India's sugarcane. This zone has fertile alluvial soil (Khadar + Bhangar), perennial irrigation from Ganga-Yamuna doab canals.

🗺️ Map Tip "SUGAR = WESTERN" — Meerut-Muzaffarnagar = Danda (sugarcane stick). West UP = Sugarcane. Doab ki sabse fertile mitti = Ganga-Yamuna doab Zone II.
22
Medium
Q22. "Bhabar" region in UP is best described as:
✅ Correct Answer: C — Porous gravelly zone where streams disappear

Bhabar is a 8-16 km wide porous, gravelly strip at the base of Himalayan foothills (Shivalik). Streams disappearing here reappear further south as springs in the Terai. Terai (Option A) = marshy, waterlogged belt with tall grasses south of Bhabar. Both Bhabar+Terai form Zone I in UP.

🗺️ Map Tip "BHABAR = BHAG JAATA HAI PAANI" — water disappears underground. Then "TERAI = TAIRA (swim)" — waterlogged, swampy. North→South: Shivalik → Bhabar → Terai → Plains.
⛏️ Minerals & Key Crops of UP
23
Hard
Q23. HINDALCO aluminium plant at Renukoot is located in which district and uses power from which source?
✅ Correct Answer: B — Sonbhadra, Rihand Hydro Power

HINDALCO (Hindustan Aluminium Corporation) at Renukoot/Renusagar is in Sonbhadra district. It's one of Asia's largest aluminium plants. It primarily uses power from Rihand Hydroelectric Project (GBP Sagar). Renusagar has a captive power plant too. Bauxite comes from Koraput (Odisha).

🗺️ Map Tip "SONBHADRA = UP ka INDUSTRIAL HUB" — HINDALCO (Renukoot), NTPC Singrauli, Obra Thermal, Rihand Dam — all Sonbhadra district. Remember: HINDALCO uses HYDRO (Rihand), NTPC uses COAL (Singrauli).
24
Medium
Q24. Which mineral is predominantly found in Mirzapur district of UP and is used in cement, glass, and iron smelting industries?
✅ Correct Answer: B — Limestone

Limestone is the most important mineral of Mirzapur and Sonbhadra (Vindhyan formation). Used in cement (Dalmia, Birla cement plants), glass industry, iron smelting (flux). Coal is found in Singrauli (Sonbhadra). Phosphorite in Sonbhadra (Bijapur mines), Silica sand in Allahabad-Mirzapur. UP has NO significant copper or bauxite deposits.

🗺️ Map Tip Vindhya region minerals: "COAL-LIME-PHOS" = Coal (Singrauli), Limestone (Mirzapur), Phosphorite (Sonbhadra). Silica = Allahabad belt. "Vindhya = Stone rich, not metal rich."
25
Hard
Q25. Consider these statements about UP's climate and select correct ones:
1. UP receives rainfall mainly from South-West monsoon via Bay of Bengal branch.   2. Rainfall decreases from East to West in UP.   3. The Vindhya region of UP experiences rain-shadow effect from Arabian Sea branch.   4. UP has a semi-arid climate in its south-western districts (Agra-Mathura-Etawah belt).
✅ Correct Answer: D — 1, 2 and 4 only

✅ Statement 1: Correct — Bay of Bengal branch brings 80%+ of UP's rain. ✅ Statement 2: Correct — East UP (Gorakhpur ~1200mm) gets more rain than West UP (Agra ~650mm). ❌ Statement 3: Vindhya region is NOT in rain shadow — it gets good rainfall (900-1100mm) from BoB branch. ✅ Statement 4: Correct — Zone IV (South-Western Semi-Arid) = Agra-Mathura-Etawah belt, <700mm annual rainfall.

🗺️ Map Tip Rainfall gradient in UP: "EAST = MORE, WEST = LESS". Gorakhpur (highest) → Lucknow → Kanpur → Agra (lowest among plains). Vindhya gets decent rain from BoB — NOT rain shadow.

📋 UP Quick Reference Facts

Essential facts for every UP Geography question — memorize these!

📐 Area

2,40,928 km²
4th largest state in India. 7.33% of India's area.

👥 Population

~24 crore (Census 2011)
Most populous state. ~16.5% of India's population.

🗺️ Districts & Divisions

75 Districts, 18 Mandals
Newest 4 districts (2011): Shamli, Hapur, Sambhal, Amethi.

🌍 Borders

9 States + Nepal
MP, Rajasthan, Haryana, Delhi, Uttarakhand, Bihar, Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, HP + Nepal.

📍 Location

24°N–30°N, 77°E–84°E
Tropic of Cancer doesn't pass through UP (passes through MP). Lies in sub-tropical zone.

🌊 Major Rivers

Ganga, Yamuna, Gomti, Ghaghara, Son, Betwa, Ken
Most rivers flow SW to NE or W to E, joining Ganga.

🦁 National Parks (1)

Dudhwa NP (1977)
Only NP in UP. Lakhimpur Kheri. ~490 sq km. Tiger + Rhino.

🐯 Tiger Reserves

Dudhwa + Pilibhit (2014)
Dudhwa TR = Dudhwa NP + Katerniaghat WLS. Pilibhit TR = 2014.

⛰️ Physical Divisions

3 Main Regions
1. Himalayan Terai (N), 2. Ganga Plain (Centre), 3. Vindhyan Upland (S).

🌾 Agro-Climatic Zones

9 Zones
Zone I (Bhabar-Terai) to Zone IX (Vindhyan). Zone IV is semi-arid (SW).

⛏️ Key Minerals

Coal, Limestone, Silica, Phosphorite
Sonbhadra = Coal (Singrauli). Mirzapur = Limestone. Allahabad = Silica sand.

💧 Largest Reservoir

Govind Ballabh Pant Sagar
Rihand Dam, Sonbhadra. India's largest man-made lake. ~10.6 BCM capacity.
