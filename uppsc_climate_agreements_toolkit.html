Climate Change — The Foundation

Greenhouse effect · GHGs · causes & impacts

Before treaties come the mechanics. UPPSC repeatedly tests the greenhouse effect, individual GHGs, and which gas does what — so anchor these cold.

🌡️ THE GREENHOUSE EFFECT — one-line definition

Trapping of outgoing infrared (long-wave) radiation by atmospheric gases, raising Earth's surface temperature. Natural greenhouse effect keeps Earth ~15°C (else ~ –18°C); the enhanced greenhouse effect from human emissions causes global warming. (UPPSC 2018 asked this verbatim — answer: "increase in global temperature".)

🧪 Major Greenhouse Gases — know the ranking
GHGMain SourceGWP / Note
Carbon dioxide (CO₂)Fossil fuels, deforestationGWP = 1 (baseline); largest contributor by volume
Methane (CH₄)Paddy fields, livestock, landfills~28–80× CO₂; main component of natural gas
Nitrous oxide (N₂O)Fertilizers, biomass burning~265× CO₂
F-gases (HFCs, PFCs, SF₆)Refrigeration, industryVery high GWP; HFCs targeted by Kigali Amendment
Water vapourNatural evaporationStrongest natural GHG (feedback, not directly controlled)
⚠️ Exam trap: Ozone (O₃) at the surface is a pollutant + GHG, but stratospheric ozone protects us. CFCs are ozone-depleting (Montreal Protocol) AND greenhouse gases — but they are not covered under the Kyoto Protocol because Montreal already handles them.

  • Causes: fossil-fuel combustion, deforestation, industrial processes, agriculture (enteric fermentation, paddy), land-use change.
  • Impacts: sea-level rise, glacial retreat (Himalayan/HKH), erratic monsoon, heatwaves, ocean acidification, coral bleaching, biodiversity loss, climate migration.
  • India-specific: Himalayan glacier melt threatens river flows; coastal Sundarbans & cyclone risk; agriculture (60%+ rain-fed) most vulnerable.

IPCC — The Science Body

Structure · reports · key findings
🔬

Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change

Est. 1988 · by WMO + UNEP · HQ Geneva · Nobel Peace Prize 2007 (shared with Al Gore)
🎯 Role
Assesses climate science — it does not conduct original research or set policy. "Policy-relevant, not policy-prescriptive."
🧩 Three Working Groups
WG-I: Physical science · WG-II: Impacts & adaptation · WG-III: Mitigation
📑 Assessment Reports
AR1 (1990) → AR6 (2021–23). AR6 Synthesis released March 2023.
🇮🇳 India link
Indian scientists contribute as authors; AR6 underlines South Asia as a climate hotspot (monsoon variability, glacier loss). India cites IPCC's equity/CBDR framing in negotiations.
Key AR6 numbers to remember: Human influence has warmed the planet ~1.1°C above pre-industrial. To stay within 1.5°C, global emissions must fall ~43% by 2030 (vs 2019) and reach net-zero CO₂ by ~2050.

UNFCCC → Kyoto → Paris: The Evolution

The single most-tested sequence

The climate-treaty family tree. The UNFCCC is the parent convention; Kyoto and Paris are its protocols/agreements. Lock this chain.

1992 · RIO

UNFCCC

Parent framework. Adopted at Rio Earth Summit; in force 1994. Goal: stabilise GHGs. Annual COPs begin (COP1 = Berlin, 1995).

➜
1997 · KYOTO

Kyoto Protocol

First binding targets — only for developed (Annex-I) nations. Top-down. In force 2005. Market mechanisms: CDM, JI, ET.

➜
2015 · PARIS

Paris Agreement

Universal — all countries pledge via NDCs. Bottom-up. In force 2016. Goal: well below 2°C, pursue 1.5°C.

⚖️ Kyoto vs Paris — the contrast UPPSC loves
FeatureKyoto Protocol (1997)Paris Agreement (2015)
ApproachTop-down, prescribed targetsBottom-up, self-determined (NDCs)
Who has targetsOnly developed (Annex-I)All parties
Binding?Targets legally bindingProcess binding, targets nationally set
Commitment periods2008–12; 2013–20 (Doha Amendment)5-year cycles, ratcheting ambition
Temperature goalNone specifiedWell below 2°C; pursue 1.5°C
⚠️ Classic trap (UPPSC 2019 actually asked): "Kyoto Protocol = Save water" is WRONG — Kyoto is about GHG emission reduction. Correct pairs: Montreal→Ozone, Stockholm→POPs, Cartagena→Biosafety.

Paris Agreement & India's NDC NEW 2031–35 NDC

1.5°C target · NDCs · India's net-zero 2070
📜

Paris Agreement

2015 · COP21, Paris · in force Nov 2016 · 190+ parties
🎯 Objective
Hold warming well below 2°C, pursue efforts toward 1.5°C above pre-industrial.
🧱 Key features
NDCs (Nationally Determined Contributions), 5-yearly Global Stocktake, finance ($100bn/yr pledge), Loss & Damage, ratchet mechanism.
📊 Global Stocktake
First one concluded at COP28 (2023, Dubai) → "UAE Consensus" calling to transition away from fossil fuels.
⚠️ US status
USA formally withdrew again in January 2026; sent no delegation to COP30.
🇮🇳 INDIA'S NDC — the headline update (March/April 2026)

India submitted its new NDC for 2031–35 to the UNFCCC (Cabinet approved 25 March 2026; submitted 24 April 2026). It builds on the 2015 original and 2022 update, keeping net-zero by 2070 unchanged.

🇮🇳 India's NDC targets — three generations
Target2015 (original)2022 updateNEW 2035 NDC
Emissions-intensity cut (vs 2005)33–35% by 203045% by 203047% by 2035
Non-fossil installed capacity40% by 203050% by 203060% by 2035
Carbon sink (GtCO₂e via forest/tree)2.5–3.02.5–3.0 by 20303.5–4.0 by 2035

🔥 High-yield for 2026: The 50% non-fossil capacity target (originally for 2030) was achieved in June 2025 — five years early. India's LT-LEDS (long-term low-carbon strategy) was submitted at COP27 (2022). Net-zero year = 2070 (NOT 2050 like many nations).
🤝 INDIA'S NEGOTIATING POSITION — write this in Mains

India anchors its stance on Common But Differentiated Responsibilities & Respective Capabilities (CBDR-RC), climate justice, equity, and low per-capita emissions. It demands developed nations honour finance + technology transfer, and champions climate finance as a right, not charity. India also leads grassroots ideas like Mission LiFE (Lifestyle for Environment, proposed COP26 Glasgow, launched June 2022).

🌳

COP30 — Belém, Brazil (Nov 2025)

10–21 Nov 2025 · the "implementation COP" · 10th anniversary of Paris
💰 Finance
Roadmap to mobilise $1.3 trillion/year by 2035 for climate action.
🛡️ Adaptation
Agreed to triple adaptation finance by 2035; adopted 59 Belém Adaptation Indicators.
🛢️ Fossil fuels
No explicit phase-out text (80+ for vs 80+ against). Brazil's Presidency launched voluntary roadmaps on fossil-fuel transition + halting deforestation.
🌲 Forests
Launch of Tropical Forests Forever Facility (TFFF) — long-term forest finance (53 countries endorsed, $5.5bn+ announced).
🇮🇳 India at COP30
India (like USA and China) did not send top leaders to the leaders' summit; reaffirmed CBDR and pushed for developed-nation finance. New 2035 NDC came shortly after, in early 2026.
Mnemonic for venues: Paris–Glasgow–Sharm–Dubai–Baku–Belém → "Please Give Some Delicious Bread & Butter" (COP21,26,27,28,29,30).

Carbon Markets, Credits & Trading

How polluters pay — and India's CCTS
  • Carbon credit: a tradable certificate = 1 tonne of CO₂-equivalent reduced/avoided.
  • Cap-and-trade: regulator sets an emissions cap; firms trade allowances. Carbon tax = a fixed price per tonne instead.
  • Kyoto mechanisms: CDM (Clean Development Mechanism — developed funds projects in developing nations), Joint Implementation, Emissions Trading. India was a top CDM host.
  • Paris Article 6: governs new international carbon markets — 6.2 (country-to-country trade) & 6.4 (centralised mechanism, successor to CDM). Rules largely finalised at COP28–COP29.
🇮🇳 INDIA — Carbon Credit Trading Scheme (CCTS)

India is building its own compliance carbon market under the Energy Conservation (Amendment) Act, 2022. The CCTS (notified 2023) replaces the older PAT/ESCerts & REC framework and creates a domestic carbon credit market regulated by the Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE).

Other Key Environmental Conventions

CBD · CITES · Ramsar · Montreal · Basel — Name | Year | Place | Objective | India
🧬

CBD — Convention on Biological Diversity

1992 · Rio Earth Summit · in force 1993
🎯 Objective
Three goals: conservation of biodiversity, sustainable use, and fair & equitable sharing of benefits from genetic resources.
🔗 Protocols
Cartagena (2000, biosafety/LMOs) · Nagoya (2010, access & benefit-sharing).
🌏 COP15 — Kunming-Montreal
2022 (China-presided, held in Montreal). Adopted the Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework (KMGBF) — the "Paris Agreement for nature": 4 goals (2050) + 23 targets (2030), incl. the famous 30×30 (protect 30% of land & seas by 2030).
🇨🇴 COP16 — Cali (2024)
"Implementation/People's COP" (Cali, Colombia, Oct–Nov 2024; resumed Rome Feb 2025). Created the Cali Fund for sharing benefits from Digital Sequence Information (DSI); strengthened Indigenous peoples' role. Failed to finalise the $200bn/yr finance strategy.
🇮🇳 India's position
Party to CBD; enacted the Biological Diversity Act, 2002 (NBA at Chennai). Updated its NBSAP aligned to KMGBF using "whole-of-government / whole-of-society" approach. Champions DSI benefit-sharing for developing nations.
🐅

CITES — Trade in Endangered Species

1973 · Washington D.C. · in force 1975 · aka Washington Convention
🎯 Objective
Ensure international trade in wild animals & plants does not threaten their survival.
📋 Appendices
I: threatened with extinction — trade banned · II: not yet threatened but regulated · III: protected in at least one country.
🏛️ Secretariat
Geneva (administered by UNEP).
🇮🇳 India's position
Party since 1976. Implements via Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972. Actively lists species (e.g., proposals on sea cucumbers, star tortoise, tokay gecko) and combats illegal wildlife trade.
🦩

Ramsar Convention — Wetlands

1971 · Ramsar, Iran · in force 1975
🎯 Objective
Conservation & "wise use" of wetlands of international importance.
🌐 Mechanisms
Ramsar Sites list; Montreux Record (sites under ecological threat).
🦆 Note
Only global treaty for a single ecosystem type. World Wetlands Day = 2 February.
🇮🇳 India's position
India has the most Ramsar sites in Asia (90+ as of 2025). First sites: Chilika (Odisha) & Keoladeo (Rajasthan), 1981. Indian sites on Montreux Record: Keoladeo & Loktak (Manipur).
🛡️

Montreal Protocol — Ozone Layer

1987 · Montreal · in force 1989 · under the Vienna Convention (1985)
🎯 Objective
Phase out Ozone-Depleting Substances (ODS) — CFCs, halons, etc.
🏆 Status
Most successful environmental treaty — universally ratified. Ozone hole is recovering.
❄️ Kigali Amendment (2016)
Adds phase-down of HFCs (powerful GHGs, used as CFC substitutes). Climate co-benefit.
🇮🇳 India's position
Party; phased out CFCs ahead of schedule. Ratified the Kigali Amendment in 2021; will phase down HFCs starting 2028 with peak around 2024–28 baseline. India Cooling Action Plan (ICAP) complements it.
☣️

Basel Convention — Hazardous Waste

1989 · Basel, Switzerland · in force 1992
🎯 Objective
Control transboundary movement of hazardous wastes & their disposal — stop dumping from rich to poor nations.
👨‍👩‍👧 "Chemicals trio"
Basel (hazardous waste) · Rotterdam (1998, PIC for chemicals) · Stockholm (2001, POPs).
🔌 Recent
Plastic waste & e-waste amendments tightened control of waste trade.
🇮🇳 India's position
Party; banned import of hazardous waste for dumping. Regulates via Hazardous & Other Wastes Rules, 2016 and E-Waste Management Rules.
One-glance memory anchor — place names = treaty names: Ramsar(Iran)=wetlands · Montreal=ozone · Basel=hazardous waste · Stockholm=POPs · Rotterdam=chemicals PIC · Cartagena=biosafety · Nagoya=ABS · Kyoto=GHG · Paris=climate.
