🌙 Chandrayaan Series — चंद्रयान शृंखला (Detailed)

Development → Key Facts → India's Significance

🌑 Chandrayaan-1 COMPLETED

Launch22 Oct 2008 | Rocket: PSLV-C11 | Sriharikota (SDSC SHAR) ObjectiveIndia's first lunar orbiter — chemical & mineralogical mapping of Moon; 100 km lunar orbit StatusMission ended Aug 2009 (contact lost); objectives ~95% achieved Significance🔑 खोज: चाँद पर पानी (H₂O / OH molecules) — Moon Impact Probe (MIP) + NASA's M3 payload ने पुष्टि की। MIP crashed near south pole — तिरंगा चाँद पर पहुँचा। India became 4th nation to reach Moon.

🌗 Chandrayaan-2 PARTIAL SUCCESS

Launch22 Jul 2019 | Rocket: GSLV Mk-III (now LVM3) ObjectiveOrbiter + Lander (Vikram) + Rover (Pragyan) — soft landing near lunar south pole Status❌ Vikram lander hard-landed (7 Sep 2019, last ~2.1 km); ✅ Orbiter still operational — high-resolution camera (OHRC) best-in-class, helped CH-3 site selection SignificanceOrbiter ki 7+ years life; lessons-learnt से "failure-based design" approach CH-3 में अपनाया गया।

🌕 Chandrayaan-3 HISTORIC SUCCESS

Launch14 Jul 2023 | Rocket: LVM3-M4 | Landing: 23 Aug 2023 ObjectiveLander (Vikram) + Rover (Pragyan) soft landing — no orbiter (propulsion module only); payload: ChaSTE, ILSA, RAMBHA, APXS, LIBS, SHAPE Status✅ 100% success — 14 Earth-days surface ops; LIBS confirmed Sulphur (S) on lunar surface; ILSA recorded seismic event; later hop experiment + propulsion module returned to Earth orbit Significance🏆 India = 1st nation to land near lunar SOUTH POLE; 4th to soft-land (after USSR, USA, China). Landing site named "Shiv Shakti Point" (Statio Shiv Shakti); 23 August = National Space Day. CH-1 crash site (2008) = "Tiranga Point".
⭐ UPPSC FAVOURITE FACTS Lander total mass ≈ 1,752 kg (rover 26 kg) • Velocity at touchdown < 2 m/s • Mission cost ≈ ₹615 crore • Project Director: P. Veeramuthuvel; Mission Director: S. Mohanakumar; tab ISRO Chief: S. Somanath (अब Chairman: V. Narayanan, Jan 2025 से)

🔮 Chandrayaan-4 & 5 (LUPEX) — Future APPROVED / DESIGN PHASE

  • Chandrayaan-4 (approved Sep 2024): lunar sample-return mission (~2027-28); 5 modules, two launches; needs in-space docking — इसीलिए SpaDeX experiment किया गया। Currently in design phase.
  • Chandrayaan-5 / LUPEX: ISRO–JAXA (Japan) joint mission — heavy rover (~350 kg) for lunar south pole water-ice exploration.
  • Roadmap (Space Vision 2047): Bharatiya Antariksh Station (BAS) by 2035, Indian crewed Moon landing by 2040.

👨‍🚀 Gaganyaan — गगनयान (Human Spaceflight)

India's first indigenous human spaceflight programme — 2026 का सबसे hot topic

🛸 Programme Overview

Objective3 astronauts (गगनयात्री) को ~400 km Low Earth Orbit में 3-दिन भेजकर सुरक्षित वापस लाना (sea recovery) RocketHLVM3 — Human-Rated LVM3 (with Crew Escape System) Crew ModuleBuilt with HAL; Environmental Control & Life Support System (ECLSS) StatusG1 UNCREWED — 2026 90%+ work complete; 8,000+ ground tests done (97% success rate); crewed flight (H1) targeted 2027

🤖 G1 Mission + Vyommitra

  • G1 (Gaganyaan-1): पहली uncrewed test flight — पहले Dec 2025 target थी, अब 2026 में slip (official: H2 2026 window; तैयारी जारी)।
  • Onboard: व्योममित्र (Vyommitra) — female half-humanoid robot; Hindi+English बोल सकती है; cabin pressure, temperature, radiation monitor करेगी; life-support systems simulate करेगी।
  • Tests: human-rated launch vehicle aerodynamics, orbital module ops, re-entry & crew-module sea recovery।
  • Sequence: G1 → G2 → G3 (uncrewed) → H1 crewed (2027). Success पर India चौथा देश बनेगा (USSR/Russia, USA, China के बाद) जो खुद इंसान अंतरिक्ष में भेजे।

🇮🇳 AXIOM-4 (June 2025) — UP CONNECTION Shubhanshu Shukla बने ISS जाने वाले पहले भारतीय (Axiom Mission-4, SpaceX Dragon "Grace"); Rakesh Sharma (1984) के बाद अंतरिक्ष जाने वाले दूसरे भारतीय। ~18-20 दिन ISS पर; microgravity experiments (incl. food/sprouting studies)। Mission Pilot role.

☀️ Aditya-L1 — भारत का पहला सौर मिशन

First Indian space-based solar observatory
Launch2 Sep 2023 | Rocket: PSLV-C57 OrbitHalo orbit around Lagrange Point L1 — पृथ्वी से ~15 lakh km (1.5 million km), Sun–Earth दूरी का ~1%; L1 से सूर्य 24×7 बिना ग्रहण दिखता है। Insertion: 6 Jan 2024. ObjectiveSolar corona, chromosphere, photosphere का अध्ययन; solar flares, Coronal Mass Ejections (CME), solar wind, space weather Payloads7 payloads — मुख्य: VELC (Visible Emission Line Coronagraph — IIA Bengaluru), SUIT (UV imaging — IUCAA Pune), ASPEX, SoLEXS, HEL1OS, PAPA, Magnetometer StatusOPERATIONAL CME observations & solar flare data देता आ रहा है (incl. major solar storm events of 2024-25) SignificanceIndia joined select group (NASA, ESA) with dedicated solar observatory; space-weather warning से satellites/power grids की सुरक्षा।
⭐ EXAM TRAP L1 पर satellite "stationary" नहीं — वह L1 के चारों ओर halo orbit में घूमता है। कुल Lagrange points = 5 (L1–L5)।

🧭 NavIC — Navigation with Indian Constellation

देसी GPS — और इसका 2026 का संकट (Mains-worthy)

📍 Core Facts

  • पुराना नाम: IRNSS (Indian Regional Navigation Satellite System)
  • Constellation design: 7 satellites — 3 GEO + 4 GSO (inclined geosynchronous)
  • Coverage: भारत + सीमा से ~1,500 km तक का क्षेत्र (regional system, GPS की तरह global नहीं)
  • Services: SPS (Standard Positioning — civilian) + RS (Restricted — military, encrypted)
  • Accuracy: ~5–20 m civilian; नई NVS series में indigenous Rubidium atomic clock + L1 band (smartphones के लिए) जोड़ा गया — NVS-01 (2023) से
  • दुनिया के अन्य systems: GPS (US), GLONASS (Russia), Galileo (EU), BeiDou (China), QZSS (Japan)

⚠️ NavIC Crisis 2025-26 (Current Affairs)

  • NVS-02 (Jan 2025, GSLV-F15 — 100th launch): satellite का valve fail — orbit raising नहीं हो सकी ⇒ unusable
  • कई पुराने IRNSS satellites (1A, 1C, 1D, 1E, 1G) की atomic clocks fail — constellation घटकर ~4 functional satellites
  • NVS-03 launch भी delay; replenishment अति-आवश्यक
  • Mains point: नौसेना/सेना, missile guidance, fishermen alerts, transport — सब NavIC पर निर्भर; navigation sovereignty दाँव पर

🏭 Private Space Sector — IN-SPACe, Skyroot, Agnikul

Space reforms 2020 → Indian Space Policy 2023 → Unicorn 2026

🏛️ Policy Architecture

  • 2020 Space Sector Reforms: निजी क्षेत्र (NGEs — Non-Governmental Entities) के लिए सेक्टर खुला
  • IN-SPACe (Indian National Space Promotion & Authorisation Centre, HQ: Ahmedabad): private players का single-window regulator/promoter — authorisation, ISRO facilities तक access
  • NSIL (NewSpace India Ltd, 2019): ISRO की commercial arm — PSLV/LVM3 commercial launches, satellite services
  • Indian Space Policy 2023: roles स्पष्ट — ISRO = R&D, NSIL = commercial, IN-SPACe = regulator-promoter
  • FDI in Space (2024): satellites में 74–100% तक automatic route; launch vehicles में 49% automatic
  • 💰 ₹1,000 crore VC Fund (Oct 2024, IN-SPACe के अंतर्गत) + ₹500 crore Technology Adoption Fund (Feb 2026)
  • 📈 Startups: 2014 में ~1 → अब 300+; funding 2025 में 2024 से 84% ↑; Indian space economy target: $44 billion by 2033

🔥 Skyroot Aerospace (Hyderabad)

  • Founders: Pawan Chandana & Naga Bharath Daka (ex-ISRO)
  • Vikram-S (18 Nov 2022, "Mission Prarambh"): अंतरिक्ष पहुँचने वाला पहला भारतीय private rocket (suborbital)
  • Vikram-I: पहला private orbital rocket — PM Modi ने Nov 2025 में unveil किया (Infinity Campus, Hyderabad के साथ); maiden flight 2026 target; all-carbon-fibre body, 3D-printed engines
  • 🦄 May 2026: भारत की पहली space-tech UNICORN ($60 mn round — GIC, Sherpalo)

⚡ Agnikul Cosmos (Chennai, IIT-Madras)

  • Founders: Srinath Ravichandran & Moin SPM
  • Agnibaan SOrTeD (30 May 2024): suborbital flight — विश्व का पहला single-piece 3D-printed (semi-cryogenic) engine — "Agnilet"
  • भारत का पहला private launchpad — "Dhanush", Sriharikota में
  • Orbital launch target: ~2026
⭐ अन्य नाम Dhruva Space (satellites), Pixxel (hyperspectral imaging), Bellatrix (propulsion), Digantara (space situational awareness)
