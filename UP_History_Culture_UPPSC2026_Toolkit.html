Ancient UP — The Sacred Heartland

Kashi · Prayagraj · Mathura · Ayodhya — Four Pillars of Civilization

🎯
UPPSC Angle: Ancient cities asked frequently as match-the-column, chronological order, and assertion-reason questions. Focus on founding myths, Vedic connections, and ASI protected status.
🕯️
Kashi (Varanasi)
Varanasi District — World's Oldest Living City
Ancient High Freq
Location: On the left bank of Ganga, between Varuna & Assi rivers (hence Va-r-Ana-si). Situated in Varanasi district.
Historical Significance:
• One of the 7 Sacred Cities (Saptapuri) of Hinduism
• Mentioned in Rigveda as Kashi — kingdom of light
• Capital of Kashi Mahajanapada (600 BCE)
• Birthplace of Tulsidas (Ramcharitmanas)
• Kabir, Ravidas spent lives here
• Famous for Banaras Gharana of music
• BHU (Banaras Hindu University) founded 1916 by Madan Mohan Malaviya
🎯 UPPSC ANGLE HRIDAY scheme covers Varanasi (confirmed in 2020 paper). Kashi Vishwanath Corridor inaugurated 2021. Varanasi — G20 venue 2023 (asked in 2023 paper).
⚠️
TRAP: "Oldest city in world" — Varanasi is often called this but Jericho is older. UPPSC asks "oldest living city in India" = Varanasi ✓
🤝
Prayagraj (Allahabad)
Prayagraj District — Triveni Sangam
Ancient Cultural Hub
Triveni Sangam: Confluence of Ganga, Yamuna & mythical Saraswati. Most sacred bathing site. Site of Kumbh Mela.
Historical Significance:
• Ancient name: Prayag (Vedic), Allahabad given by Akbar in 1583
• Akbar built Allahabad Fort (1583) at Sangam
• Treaty of Allahabad 1765 — signed between Robert Clive & Mughal Emperor Shah Alam II (UPPSC 2019 asked!)
• 1857 revolt's UP government seat briefly
• High Court established 1866 (oldest HC in UP)
• Nehru family home — Anand Bhavan
• Renamed Prayagraj in 2018
🎯 UPPSC ANGLE Treaty of Allahabad (1765) = Diwani rights to East India Company for Bengal, Bihar, Orissa. Kumbh 2025 held here. Prayagraj Airport — International status in 2020 paper!
🦚
Mathura
Mathura District — Birthplace of Krishna
Ancient Pilgrimage
Religious Core: Birthplace of Lord Krishna (Mathura). Part of Braj Bhoomi — includes Mathura, Vrindavan, Govardhan, Barsana, Gokul.
Historical Significance:
• Capital of Shura-sena Mahajanapada
• Mathura School of Art — flourished 1st–3rd CE (Kushana period)
• Red/spotted sandstone sculptures, earliest Buddha images in human form
• Major Buddhist, Jain & Hindu center simultaneously
• Sacked by Mahmud of Ghazni (1018 CE)
• HRIDAY scheme city (confirmed 2020 paper)
• Mathura Refinery (IOC) — important economically
🎯 UPPSC ANGLE Mathura School vs Gandhara School: Mathura = Indian tradition, red sandstone, UP origin. Gandhara = Greco-Buddhist, NW India. Common MCQ trap!
🏹
Ayodhya
Ayodhya District — Ram Janmabhoomi
Ancient Recent Current
Historical Significance:
• Capital of Kosala Mahajanapada
• Ancient name: Saket (mentioned in Buddhist texts)
• Birthplace of Lord Rama (per Valmiki Ramayana)
• Birthplace of Jain Tirthankaras: Rishabhanath (1st), Ajitanath (2nd), Abhinandannath (4th), Sumatinath (5th), Anantanath (14th)
• Fa-Hien (Chinese pilgrim, 4th CE) visited
• Ram Mandir inaugurated January 22, 2024
• Pushyamitra Shunga's Ashwamedha Yagna info from Ayodhya Inscription (asked UPPSC 2018!)
🎯 UPPSC ANGLE Ayodhya inscription → Pushyamitra Shunga's Ashwamedha Yagna. Saket = ancient name. 5 Jain Tirthankara birthplaces in Ayodhya. Ram Mandir 2024 = current affairs angle.
⚠️
TRAP: Pushyamitra Shunga Ashwamedha info comes from AYODHYA inscription (not Sarnath/Besnagar). UPPSC 2018 confirmed.

Ancient UP — Timeline

~3000 BCE
Vedic Period — UP as Core Region
Rigveda mentions Kashi. Yamuna-Ganga doab settled. Copper Hoard Culture in UP.
600 BCE
Mahajanapada Era
Kashi, Kosala (Ayodhya capital), Vatsa (Kaushambi/Allahabad), Shursena (Mathura) — 4 of 16 Mahajanapadas in UP region.
321–185 BCE
Mauryan Empire
Pataliputra capital. Ashoka's pillars at Sarnath (Lion Capital), Allahabad Pillar. Buddhism spread from Varanasi.
1st–3rd CE
Kushana Period — Mathura Art School
Mathura School of Art flourished. First standing Buddha images. Capital: Mathura.
320–550 CE
Gupta Golden Age
Prayag Prashasti (Samudragupta's victories). Nalanda near UP. Sanskrit literature peak. Kalidasa flourished.
606–647 CE
Harsha's Empire
Capital at Kanauj (UP). Xuanzang's famous visit. Prayag Mela (Kumbh precursor) held every 5 years.

Kaushambi — The Forgotten Capital

Kaushambi (Allahabad district): Capital of Vatsa Mahajanapada. Udayana was famous king. Both Buddhist (Buddha visited) and Jain significance. Ghoshitarama Monastery excavated. UPPSC frequently asks about Vatsa Mahajanapada capital.

Buddhist Sites in UP

Sarnath · Kushinagar · Shravasti · Sankisa — Buddha's Life Circuit in UP

ℹ️
Key Fact: UP contains sites related to 3 of the 4 most sacred events in Buddha's life: 1st Sermon (Sarnath), Mahaparinirvana (Kushinagar), and major teachings (Shravasti). Only Bodh Gaya (Bihar) is outside UP.
Site District Buddhist Event Key Monument UPPSC Angle
Sarnath Varanasi Dhammachakka Pavattana — First Sermon (Deer Park). Sangha founded here Dhamekh Stupa (500 CE), Ashoka Pillar (Lion Capital = National Emblem) National Emblem from here. Sarnath inscription: Pushyamitra Shunga asked UPPSC 2018
Kushinagar Kushinagar Mahaparinirvana (483 BCE) — Buddha's death Nirvana Stupa, Reclining Buddha statue (1500 yr old), Ramabhar Stupa (cremation site) International Airport status granted June 2020 (asked UPPSC 2020!). UNESCO Tentative list
Shravasti Shravasti Buddha spent 24 rainy seasons here. Jetavana Monastery founded by Anathapindika Jetavana Monastery ruins, Saheth-Maheth site, Angulimala stupa Capital of Kosala kingdom (Prasenajit's kingdom). Shravasti among districts with 20%+ forest cover (asked 2025 paper!)
Sankisa (Sankasia) Farrukhabad Buddha descended from Trayastrimsha heaven (after teaching mother Mahamaya). Ashoka built elephant pillar Ashoka's Elephant Capital Pillar (remains), stupa Least known but important. 4th Buddhist pilgrimage site in UP after above three
Kapilavastu Siddharthnagar Buddha's birthplace (Lumbini nearby in Nepal), early life spent here Piprahwa stupa (contains Buddha's ashes), identified as Kapilavastu Dispute: Piprahwa (UP) vs Tilaurakot (Nepal) — both claim Kapilavastu
Kaushambi Allahabad/Prayagraj Buddha spent 2 rainy seasons. Ghoshitarama monastery Fort ruins, Ashoka Pillar, Ghoshitarama monastery Vatsa capital. Also Jain significance

Sarnath — Detailed Analysis

Dhamekh Stupa: Built 500 CE (Gupta period) at exact spot of First Sermon. Cylindrical, 28m diameter, 43m height. Inscriptions in Brahmi script.
Chaukhandi Stupa: Marks where Buddha met his first 5 disciples. Octagonal Mughal tower added by Humayun's son Govardhan.
Ashoka Pillar: Only intact Mauryan pillar in UP. Lion Capital removed to Sarnath Museum (became National Emblem 1950).
🎯 SARNATH MCQ TRAPS Q: Where is National Emblem originally from? → Sarnath (not Delhi)
Q: Dhamekh Stupa built in which period? → Gupta (500 CE), not Maurya
Q: First sermon location? → Deer Park, Isipatana, Sarnath
Q: Sarnath inscription mentions? → Ashoka's Schism Edict for Sangha
⚠️
Sarnath Museum: Oldest site museum in India (est. 1910). Contains Lion Capital (4 lions + dharma chakra).

Buddhist Circuit in UP — 4 Major Sites

Government's Buddhist Circuit: Part of Ministry of Tourism's "Spiritual Circuit" under Swadesh Darshan scheme. Covers: Sarnath → Kushinagar → Shravasti → Sankisa. Kushinagar airport made international to boost Buddhist tourism (Oct 2021 formally opened).

Medieval UP — Mughals & Nawabs

Agra as Mughal Capital · Awadh Nawabs · Architecture & Culture

🏰
Agra — Mughal Capital
Agra District
Medieval High Freq
Sikandar Lodi shifted capital Delhi→Agra (1504). First capital shift to Agra.
Mughal Period:
• Babur: Won Battle of Panipat (1526), died in Agra, buried in Agra (later Kabul)
• Akbar: Built Agra Fort (red sandstone, 1565). Capital also at Fatehpur Sikri (1571-1585)
• Jahangir: Itmad-ud-Daula (his father-in-law's tomb) — "Baby Taj", first marble-inlay tomb
• Shah Jahan: TAJ MAHAL (1632–1653), Agra Fort's marble sections
• Aurangzeb: Moved capital to Aurangabad
🎯 UPPSC ANGLE Battle of Bilgram (1540) near Kannauj — Humayun defeated by Sher Shah. Sher Shah's tomb in Sasaram, Bihar. INA trial at Red Fort Delhi (not Agra Fort) — common trap!
⚠️
TRAP (UPPSC 2019): INA officers' trial was held at Red Fort, Delhi — NOT Agra Fort. Agra Fort was used as prison.
🕌
Taj Mahal
Agra — UNESCO World Heritage
Built: 1632–1653 by Shah Jahan for wife Mumtaz Mahal (died 1631 in Burhanpur). Chief architect: Ustad Ahmad Lahori. 20,000 workers.
  • White Makrana marble from Rajasthan
  • Pietra dura (inlay work) technique
  • Perfect symmetry — 4 identical facades
  • Four minarets lean slightly outward (earthquake protection)
  • Yomuna river on north side
  • UNESCO WHS since 1983
  • New 7 Wonders of World (2007)
⚠️
Note: Ustad Isa Khan was chief calligrapher, NOT architect. Common confusion in MCQs.

Lucknow — Nawabi Architecture
Awadhi Style Heritage
Bada Imambara (1784): Built by Asaf-ud-Daula during famine relief. Largest arched hall in Asia without beams. Bhool Bhulaiya (maze) inside.
  • Chota Imambara (1838): Nawab Muhammad Ali Shah's tomb. "Palace of Lights"
  • Rumi Darwaza: 18th century gateway, model for Ottoman-style arch
  • Residency: British Residency — site of 1857 Siege of Lucknow (5 months)
  • Constantia (La Martiniere): Claude Martin's palace, now school
  • Ganga-Jamuni Tehzeeb: Lucknow's syncretic Hindu-Muslim culture, Urdu, Kathak dance, nawabi cuisine (biryani, kebabs)

Fatehpur Sikri
Agra District — Akbar's Capital
Mughal UPPSC Fav
Built: 1571–1585 by Akbar. Capital for 14 years, abandoned (water scarcity). UNESCO WHS 1986.
  • Buland Darwaza: Built after Gujarat victory 1576. 54m height. "Gateway to victory"
  • Panch Mahal: 5-storey pavilion, inspired by Buddhist viharas
  • Jama Masjid: Mosque for court
  • Dargah of Salim Chishti: Akbar's son Jahangir born after saint's blessing
  • Diwan-i-Khas: Hall of private audience, 36 pillars, central column
🕰️
Jaunpur — Sharqi Kingdom
Jaunpur District — Shiraz-i-Hind
Sharqi Dynasty (1394–1479): Founded by Malik Sarwar (Khwaja Jahan). Independent sultanate. Called "Shiraz of India" for culture. Ibrahim Shah Sharqi: patron of arts, music.
  • Atala Mosque (1408): Unique style — high screen facade (UPPSC 2019 asked chronological order!)
  • Jami Masjid: Largest Sharqi mosque
  • Lal Darwaza Mosque: Built by Ibrahim Shah's queen
  • Shahi Pul (Bridge): 16th century Mughal bridge
⚠️
TRAP (2019): Atala Mosque Jaunpur (1408) → Humayun's Tomb Delhi (1565) → Rabia Daurani Aurangabad (1678). Chronological order: Jaunpur < Delhi < Aurangabad.

1857 — First War of Independence in UP

Meerut · Lucknow · Jhansi · Kanpur — UP at the Centre of Revolt

🔥
Most Asked UP Topic! UPPSC 2023 asked: Start dates of revolt at different places. UPPSC 2023 asked: Leader of Bareilly revolt 1857. UPPSC 2021: Ram Prasad Bismil birth anniversary in Shahjahanpur. Know ALL locations, dates and leaders.

Causes of 1857 Revolt — UP Specific

Military Causes (Immediate):
Enfield rifle cartridges greased with cow & pig fat. Sepoys had to bite the cartridge. Hindu & Muslim soldiers both outraged.
Political Causes (UP-specific):
• Awadh annexed 1856 by Dalhousie under "misgovernance"
• Doctrine of Lapse → Satara, Jhansi, Nagpur (NOT Awadh)
• Displacement of Awadh's court, army, nobility
• Nana Saheb denied pension after Peshwa Baji Rao II's death
Economic Causes:
• Exploitation through Permanent Settlement, Ryotwari
• Ruin of artisans (handloom), zamindars
• Doctrine of Lapse hurt ruling families
Social/Religious Causes:
• Fear of conversion to Christianity
• Widow Remarriage Act (1856)
• Western education seen as attack on culture
• Greased cartridge = religious insult

Key Centers in UP — Dates & Leaders

Place (District)Date of RevoltLeaderKey EventsUPPSC Trap

Kanpur (Cawnpore) June 1857 Nana Saheb (Dhondu Pant) Massacre at Bibighar (British women & children killed). British forces under Havelock recaptured Nana Saheb = Kanpur (NOT Lucknow). Tatya Tope was Nana Saheb's general
Jhansi 11 May 1857 Rani Lakshmi Bai Fought British forces under Hugh Rose. Died fighting at Kotah-ki-Serai (Gwalior) 17 June 1858 Rani died near Gwalior (MP), NOT Jhansi! Jhansi district is UP
Bareilly 1857 Khan Bahadur Khan Declared himself Nawab Nazim of Rohilkhand. Major center in UP UPPSC 2023 directly asked: Bareilly 1857 leader = Khan Bahadur Khan ✓
Faizabad/Awadh June 1857 Maulvi Ahmadullah Shah "Maulvi of Faizabad" — fierce fighter. Had predicted revolt. Killed 1858 for reward money Maulvi Ahmadullah = Faizabad (Awadh region), NOT Lucknow center

Why 1857 Revolt Failed — Key Reasons

Military Failure:
• No unified command structure
• Bombay & Madras armies largely stayed loyal
• Better British weapons (Enfield rifles used by British forces)
• Sikh soldiers & Gurkhas fought FOR British
Political Failure:
• No common leader
• Regional character — not pan-India
• Educated middle class, merchants didn't join
• Feudal nature of movement (zamindars, nawabs)
⚠️
KEY TRAPS in 1857 MCQs:
1. Doctrine of Lapse ≠ cause for Awadh annexation (Awadh = "misgovernance")
2. Mangal Pandey's revolt = Barrackpore (Bengal), not UP
3. Rani Lakshmi Bai died at Gwalior (Kotah-ki-Serai), not Jhansi
4. Lucknow leader = Hazrat Mahal (not Nana Saheb who was Kanpur)
5. Bareilly leader = Khan Bahadur Khan (UPPSC 2023 confirmed)

UP After Independence

Formation · Reorganization · States Reorganization Act 1956

Formation & Reorganization Timeline

1902
United Provinces of Agra and Oudh
Created by British by merging NW Provinces & Oudh. Lucknow made capital. Called "United Provinces" (UP).
26 Jan 1950
Renamed "Uttar Pradesh"
When India became Republic, United Provinces renamed Uttar Pradesh. Govind Ballabh Pant was first Chief Minister (1950).
1956
States Reorganization Act
UP boundaries largely retained on linguistic basis. Hindi-speaking belt unified under UP.
9 Nov 2000
Uttarakhand (Uttaranchal) Carved Out
13 hill districts of UP formed new state Uttaranchal (renamed Uttarakhand 2007). 27th state of India.

Mathura-Vrindavan — Braj Culture
Mathura District
Cultural Heritage
Braj Mandal: 84-Kos Parikrama region — includes Mathura, Vrindavan, Govardhan, Barsana, Nandgaon, Gokul.
  • Holi of Barsana: Lathmar Holi — women beat men with sticks. Unique tradition
  • Janmashtami: Krishna's birth night celebrated grandly in Mathura & Vrindavan
  • Brij Utsav: Annual cultural festival
  • Raslila: Traditional dance drama on Krishna's life in Vrindavan
  • Banke Bihari Temple: Most visited in Vrindavan
  • ISKCON Temple: Major global pilgrimage center in Vrindavan
  • Govardhan Puja: Day after Diwali — community celebrates Govardhan hill
💃
Kathak Dance
Classical Dance — UP Origin
Classical Art
Origin: "Katha" (story) + "kathakas" (storytellers). Temple dancers of North India → Mughal court → refined as Kathak. Two main gharanas from UP:
  • Lucknow Gharana: Nawab Wajid Ali Shah patronized. Soft, lyrical, sensuous. Emphasis on bhava (expression). Bindadin Maharaj lineage.
  • Jaipur Gharana: More vigorous, emphasis on taal, tandav. (Note: Jaipur is Rajasthan but historically linked)
  • Birju Maharaj (Lucknow Gharana): Greatest Kathak maestro of 20th century
Other UP Classical Arts:
• Thumri: Semi-classical vocal — Varanasi & Lucknow centers
• Tappa: Refined by Miya Shori in Muhammad Shah's court (asked UPPSC 2023!)
• Dadra, Chaiti, Kajri: Folk-classical forms of UP

Literary Heritage of UP
Bhakti, Sufi & Modern Literature
  • Tulsidas (Varanasi): Ramcharitmanas (1574). Greatest Hindi literary work. Awadhi dialect.
  • Kabir (Varanasi/Magahar): 15th cent. Sant-poet. Dohas (couplets). Died Magahar (Gorakhpur).
  • Surdas (Mathura): Sursagar — Krishna devotional poetry. Blind poet. Ashtachap group of Vallabha sect.
  • Mirabai (Vrindavan): Though born Rajasthan, spent later life Vrindavan. Krishna bhakti.
  • Premchand (Lamhi, Varanasi): "Upanyas Samrat." Godan, Nirmala, Gaban. Modern Hindi/Urdu literature.
  • Harivansh Rai Bachchan (Prayagraj): Madhushala. Father of Amitabh Bachchan.
  • Mahadevi Verma (Farrukhabad): "Modern Meera." Chhayavad poet. Sahitya Akademi, Padma Vibhushan.
