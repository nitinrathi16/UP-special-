🗺️ 9 Agro-Climatic Zones of UP
Zone → Districts → Crops → Problems (UPPSC Mains mein bahut important!)
📌 Exam Tip
UP has 9 agro-climatic zones (as per UP Agriculture Dept). India level par UP comes under 3 national zones — Upper Gangetic Plains (Zone 5), Middle Gangetic Plains (Zone 4), and partly Bundelkhand region. Zone 6 (Bundelkhand) and Zone 9 (Vindhyachal) are drought-prone — frequently asked!
Zone 1 — Tarai & Bhabhar Region High rainfall 900–1100mm
📍 Saharanpur, Bahraich, Shravasti, Lakhimpur Kheri, Pilibhit, Bijnor (northern belt near Nepal)
Rice Wheat Sugarcane Maize Jute
Marshy/forest belt; waterlogging problem; wells & canals; high biological diversity
Zone 2 — Western Plain Region Sub-humid | 800–1000mm
📍 Meerut, Ghaziabad, Muzaffarnagar, Bulandshahr, Aligarh, Mathura, Agra, Hapur
Wheat Sugarcane Potato Rice Cotton
Most fertile zone; khadar & bhangar alluvial soils; Upper Ganga Canal; highest agricultural intensity
Zone 3 — Central Western Plain Region Sub-humid | 800–950mm
📍 Moradabad, Bareilly, Rampur, Shahjahanpur, Pilibhit, Budaun, Sambhal
Sugarcane Wheat Rice Mustard Pulses
Major sugar mill cluster; Ramganga multipurpose irrigation project; fertile Rohilkhand plains
Zone 4 — South Western Semi-Arid Region Semi-arid | 600–800mm
📍 Agra, Mathura (overlap), Firozabad, Etah, Mainpuri, Farrukhabad, Kannauj, Auraiya
Potato Wheat Bajra Mustard Asafoetida
Potato capital (Agra-Farrukhabad); less rainfall; tube well irrigation dependent; IPC Centre at Aligarh
Zone 5 — Central Plain Region Sub-humid | 800–1000mm
📍 Lucknow, Unnao, Barabanki, Raebareli, Sitapur, Hardoi, Fatehpur, Kaushambi
Wheat Rice Mango Pulses Sugarcane
Malihabad mango (Dussehri); Sharda Sahayak canal; state capital zone; diverse cropping
Zone 6 — Bundelkhand Region Drought-prone | 700–900mm
📍 Jhansi, Banda, Mahoba, Chitrakoot, Hamirpur, Jalaun, Lalitpur
Jowar Gram Til (Sesame) Bajra Urad
Rocky Vindhyan terrain; drought every 2-3 years; Ken-Betwa River Linking Project (India's 1st) for relief; backward region
Zone 7 — North Eastern Plain Region Humid | 1000–1200mm
📍 Gorakhpur, Deoria, Kushinagar, Maharajganj, Basti, Siddharth Nagar, Sant Kabir Nagar
Rice Wheat Banana Sugarcane Maize
Nepal border zone; Gandak canal (Indo-Nepal project); Gorakhpur-Deoria sugar mills; banana production (Kushinagar)
Zone 8 — Eastern Plain Region Humid | 1000–1200mm
📍 Varanasi, Prayagraj, Azamgarh, Jaunpur, Ghazipur, Ballia, Mirzapur, Sonbhadra, Chandauli, Bhadohi
Rice Wheat Guava Amla Vegetables
Allahabad guava (GI tag); Pratapgarh amla; Eastern Ganga canal; Carpet industry (Bhadohi, Mirzapur)
Zone 9 — Vindhyachal Region Plateau region | Low fertility
📍 Mirzapur (plateau part), Sonbhadra, Robertsganj
Jowar Til Gram Rice
Plateau/hill; Rihand reservoir; tribal belt; lowest cultivated %; minerals (coal, bauxite, limestone)

🌊 Major Canal Systems of UP
🏛️
Upper Ganga Canal (Ganga Nahar)
Built 1854 (British era) — India's oldest major canal. Starts at Haridwar, flows through Meerut, Aligarh, Mathura, Agra divisions. Also called "Upper Doab Canal".
🌊
Lower Ganga Canal
From Narora (Bulandshahr) to Fatehpur; irrigates Etah, Farrukhabad, Kanpur, Fatehpur districts. Built 1878.
💧
Sharda Canal (Sarda Nahar)
Originates from Banbasa (Uttarakhand); major north UP canal. Irrigates Lakhimpur Kheri, Sitapur, Hardoi, Barabanki, Gonda, Bahraich. One of the largest canal systems in India.
🔗
Sharda Sahayak Canal (Sarda Sahayak Pariyojana)
Links Sharda with Ganga river system. Irrigates Balrampur, Gonda, Bahraich. Major Tarai region project. Reduces monsoon dependence.
🤝
Gandak Canal (UP–Bihar Joint Project)
Indo-Nepal joint multipurpose project. Irrigates Deoria, Gorakhpur, Kushinagar in eastern UP. Also benefits Bihar.
🚿
Agra Canal
From Okhla Barrage (Delhi) to Agra. Multipurpose — irrigation + drinking water for Agra city.
⚡
Ramganga Multipurpose Project
Dam at Kalagarh, Uttarakhand. Irrigates Moradabad, Bareilly, Shahjahanpur. Also generates hydroelectricity. Ramganga river (tributary of Ganga).
🌉
Ken-Betwa River Linking Project
India's first river-linking project. Ken → Betwa (UP–MP joint). Target: 10.62 lakh hectares in Bundelkhand. Approved; helps drought-prone Jhansi, Banda, Mahoba districts.
🏞️
Rihand Dam (Govind Ballabh Pant Sagar)
On Rihand river, Sonbhadra. India's largest reservoir by volume (at time of construction). Hydro power + irrigation for Vindhyachal zone.
